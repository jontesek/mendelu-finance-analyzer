    Second day of put selling in ADT

    By Mike Yamamoto (mike.yamamoto@optionmonster.com) 3 hours ago
    • 
    • 
    • 
    • 
    Content preferences

      Traders are looking for home-security company ADT to hold its ground after quarterly results at the end of this month.

      More than 12,000 August 31 puts were sold for $0.55 yesterday, according to optionMONSTER's tracking systems. This represents new positioning, as volume was triple the previous open interest in the strike.

      The traders collect the premium from the option sales and are looking for ADT to stay above $31 through expiration five weeks from today. If the stock falls below that strike price, these put sellers will be on the hook to buy shares at that level. Investors sometimes sell puts this way in hopes of picking up a stock at an effective discount to existing levels. (See our Education section)

      This follows selling in the August 35 puts in the previous session , in a roll from July contracts.

      ADT slipped 0.15 percent to $32.61 yesterday. Shares gapped down from $40 after the company's last earnings report in late April but have been trying to hold support at current levels in the last week. The next quarterly numbers are scheduled for July 29.

      Yesterday's heavy put selling pushed ADT's total option volume above 17,000, nearly 9 times its daily average for the last month.
