    Advanced Micro Devices Analyst Roundup Following Q4 Results

    By Kevin Riley January 22, 2015 6:45 AM
    • 
    • 
    • 
    • 
    Content preferences

      Advanced Micro Devices, Inc. (NASDAQ: AMD) reported Q4 results inline with expectations Tuesday and the stock surged 9.38 percent Wednesday to $2.45.

      Analysts commented on the stock following the earnings release. Below are highlights along with current ratings and price targets.

      Ascendiant Capital Markets - Buy, $4.50 price target

      With a well known inventory correction underway in AMDs consumer desktop and graphics markets, as well as typical end-of-year consumption of console components, AMD reported Q4 numbers that were generally in-line with expectations. The company gave guidance for the tail-end of the inventory clean up in Q1, before returning to a more stable computing and graphics market in Q2. While March quarter guidance is below the prior consensus, we believe Street expectations were already fairly pessimistic and that AMDs call for FY15 profitability and a turn into the June quarter is likely enough to at least support AMDs shares near its current level.

      FBR & Co. - Market Perform, $3.50 price target

      Overall for AMD, in our view, the company has wisely directed its strategic focus away from the core PC market and toward gaming APUs, microservers, and custom-embedded processors, areas on which its larger competitor is less focused. However, we now believe much of this transition has already played out, with visibility into meaningful peak earnings elusive.

      Credit Suisse - Underperform, $2.00 price target

      While AMD continues to manage expenses admirably OpEx declined ~11 percent y/y in 2014 and ~55 percent from its peak in C1Q08 we continue to struggle with unfavorable structural headwinds: (1) Gaming likely peaked in 2014, (2) advantages of HSA still unclear and 2 semi-custom designs unlikely to ramp before 2016 (~$300m/year), (3) share loss in core PCs AMD computing rev was down $370m y/y in 2H14 or ~10-15m units and (4) AMD is quickly loosing scale with a less defined Moores Law road-map and an R&D budget down 50 percent since 2008 and 1/10 of INTCs.

      Morgan Stanley - Underweight, $2.25 price target

      Another difficult quarter for AMD on all revenue lines. The PC centric computing segment is contracting at a surprising rate, with 15% sequential declines in both 4q and 1q; this segment has declined 44 percent since 2Q13 in a solid PC environment, and the company wrote down some of it's production for lower of cost or market. The console business was basically in line with our expectations for 4q, but will be down y/y in 1q, and we continue to see minimal growth from the year in that segment. We believe AMD shipped about 24 mm units into consoles last year, about the peak single unit year from years 1-5 of last generation consoles, and we would expect small unit growth in 2015 to be offset by price declines.

      Jefferies - Buy, $4.00 price target

      On the positive side, AMD has a broad set of unique IP (x86, ARM, GPU, server-fabric) that it appears to finally figured out how to monetize. In 2014 AMD successfully grew its non-PC business (Enterprise, Embedded and Semi-Custom, or EE&S), by 50 percent to 43 percent of its total revenues at 17 percent operating margins. In 2015, we expect continued growth driven by consoles and embedded, and we think that the company could ship an x86/ARM hybrid processor into a network-function virtualization market. In 2016, we expect the AMD to start shipping two new high volume semi-custom chips with lifetime revenues of up to $1 billion.
